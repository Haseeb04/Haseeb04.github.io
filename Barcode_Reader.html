Barcode Reader

A barcode is a means of rapresenting data in machine-readable form. Traditional barcodes rapresent data by a aset of parallel lines of varied width and spacing.
Principles of operation of a Barcode:
  • 1) A light from a laser illuminates the barcode.
  • 2) More light is reflected from the white areas than from the black bars.
  • 3) The reflected light is captured by one or more photoelectric cells that generate a set of electrical pulses that correspond to the black and white stripes in the barcode.
  • 4) These pulses are processed and converted to a binary number that rapresents the code.
  • 5) Check digit.
Most barcode readers indicate that the barcode has been read successfully, for example by sounding a beep or showing a green light.
Calculate the check digit of a Barcode
